Fall 2025 Conference

Ancient Places, Living Histories: Stones, Stories, and Settlement

November 7 - 9 at Bridgewater State University, Massachusetts

The Fall 2025 NEARA Conference will be at the Moakley Center Auditorium of Bridgewater State University in Bridgewater Massachusetts on the second weekend of November. We have reserved a block of rooms at the Holiday Inn Express in Middleborough MA.

The Bridgewater area is rich in history and prehistory, with weirs, grinding stones, petroglyphs, stone walls and chambers, historic inscriptions, pulpit rocks, "praying village" sites, and many landmarks associated with King Philip's War.

View the Fall 2025 Program.

Conference Speakers

Speakers will include:

  • Matt McLaughlin, Tracing History Through Stones in Plymouth County, MA
  • Jonathan Scott, The Nature of Sacred Sites
  • Malissa Costa and Danielle Alonso, The Wampanoag's Relationship with the Natural World
  • Kevin Gardner, Discovering New England Stone Walls
  • Peter Anick, Petroglyphs of the Northeastern Woodlands
  • Nathaniel Kitchel, An Introduction to Lithic Sourcing

Field Trips

Friday field trips are currently planned for King Philip's Rock, for Indian Head River, plus an explore-on-your-own-schedule tour of the Bridgewater area.

Sunday field trips are currently planned for Middleborough and Lakeville, for Foxborough State Forest, and for Bournedale and Plymouth.

Field trip information will be emailed to conference registrants about one week before the event.
